The graph is interactive: you can click and drag an empty place to move. You can scroll to zoom in or out. You can click and drag a node to move it.
  • Black rectangle represents the environment, i.e. the exogenous nutrients.
  • Blue rectangles are the Level 0 trophic groups. Level 0 groups are primary consumers : they only uptake exogenous nutrients. Each group gathers one or more cells together.
  • Green rectangles are the Level 1 trophic groups. Level 1 groups are secondary consumers : they uptake exogenous nutrients, and also nutrients produced by other groups. Each group gathers one or more cells together.
  • Orange rectangles are the Level 2 trophic groups. Level 2 groups are secondary consumers : they only uptake nutrients produced by other groups. Each group gathers one or more cells together.
  • Grey rectangles are the trophic groups that not belong to any level (No level). No level groups do not uptake anything. Each group clusterize one or more cells.
Black edges show necrophagy activity. Blue edges show uptake activity linked to an active release of the prey.
The name of each node is displayed as following:
  • Trophic group names represent the uptaken nutrients, followed by the number of cells belonging to this group, and its lifespan. For example, "1-2 (20, 50)" is the trophic group uptaking nutrients 1 and 2, gathering 20 cells together, and with a lifespan of 50.
  • The environment name represents the list of the exogenous nutrients. For example, "ENV (1-2)" indicates that the environment displays exogenous nutrients 1 and 2.
