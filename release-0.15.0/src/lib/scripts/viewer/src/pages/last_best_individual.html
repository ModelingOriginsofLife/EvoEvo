Best genetic regulation network Top


The graph is interactive: you can click and drag an empty place to move. You can scroll to zoom in or out. You can click and drag a node to move it. Groups of nodes, such as 'Transcription factors' or 'Co-enzymes', can also be moved as a unit.
  • Green triangles are the co-enzymes (CoE). In brackets is indicated what type of metabolite is the co-enzyme,
  • Orange rectangles are the transcription factors (TF). They belong to 4 types: A is activated by its co-enzyme ; R is repressed by its co-enzyme ; AA is always active ; AI is always inactive. Transcription factors can be expressed in the genome (NI) or inherited epigenetically through the cytoplasm (I),
  • Blue circles are the metabolic enzymes (E). The reaction performed by the enzyme is indicated in brackets. "-" symbolizes an external metabolite (i.e. the enzyme is a pump).
Solid edges are for activation or upregulation. Dashed edges are for inhibition or downregulation.

Best metabolic network Top


The graph is interactive: you can click and drag an empty place to move. You can scroll to zoom in or out. You can click and drag a node to move it. Groups of nodes, such as 'Cell', can also be moved as a unit.
  • Blue circles are the non essential metabolites. They are part of the metabolic network, but do not contribute directly to the fitness,
  • Green rectangles are the essential metabolites. They contribute directly to the fitness.
  • Negative nodes are the metabolites that are external to the cell, and are pump in or out by the metabolic network.
Solid edges are for reactions that are currently flowing. Dashed edges are for inactive enzymatic reactions.
